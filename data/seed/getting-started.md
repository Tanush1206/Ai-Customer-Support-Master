# Getting Started with Nimbus

Welcome to Nimbus! This guide walks you through everything you need to go from a brand-new account to syncing, organizing, and sharing your files across all your devices. Follow the steps in order, or jump to the section you need.

## Create your account

Getting started takes just a couple of minutes.

1. Go to the Nimbus sign-up page and enter your email address and a password.
2. Confirm your email when the verification message arrives.
3. Sign in to the web app to land on your file dashboard.

Every new account starts on the **Free** plan, which includes **5 GB of storage**, a maximum file size of **2 GB**, and **30-day version history and trash**. That's plenty to explore the basics.

When you're ready for more room, you can upgrade anytime:

- **Plus** — $11.99/mo (or $9.99/mo billed annually) for **2 TB** of storage, **50 GB** max file size, **180-day** version history, password-protected and expiring links, and Smart Sync.
- **Family** — $19.99/mo for up to **6 members** with **2 TB each**, plus parental controls and all Plus features.
- **Business** — from $18/user/mo (annual, minimum 3 users) with **5 TB pooled per user**, an admin console, SSO/SAML, audit logs, and team folders.

Annual billing saves about 17% (roughly two months free), and your first paid plan is backed by a **14-day money-back refund**. Manage everything later under **Settings → Billing**.

### Secure your account

Before you add files, take a moment to turn on two-factor authentication (2FA). Nimbus supports an **authenticator app (TOTP)**, **SMS**, or a **hardware security key (FIDO2)**. When you enable 2FA, you'll receive **backup codes** — save these somewhere safe in case you lose your device. Your data is protected with **AES-256 encryption at rest** and **TLS 1.2+ in transit**.

## Install the desktop app

The Nimbus desktop app keeps a local **"Nimbus" folder** on your computer that stays in sync with the cloud. It's available for **Windows, macOS, and Linux**.

1. Download and install the app for your operating system.
2. Sign in with your Nimbus account.
3. Choose a location for your Nimbus folder when prompted.

Once installed, anything you drop into the Nimbus folder syncs automatically. You can control how syncing works from the **menu-bar (macOS) or tray (Windows) icon**, including pause and resume.

A few features worth knowing about:

- **Selective Sync** lets you choose which folders download to this computer, saving local disk space.
- **Smart Sync** (Plus and above) keeps files online-only until you open them.
- **LAN sync** speeds up transfers between devices on the same network.

If two devices edit the same file while offline, Nimbus creates a **"conflicted copy"** so no work is lost.

## Upload your first files

You can add files from the web or the desktop app.

**From the web:**

1. Open the Nimbus web app.
2. Click **Upload**, or drag files directly onto the page.
3. Wait for the upload to finish — your files appear in your file list right away.

**From the desktop app:**

1. Open your local Nimbus folder.
2. Copy or move files into it, just like any other folder.
3. The app syncs them to the cloud in the background.

Keep your plan's limits in mind: the Free plan caps individual files at **2 GB**. Remember that storage is shared across all your files, backups, and Nimbus Paper docs.

## Organize with folders

A little structure now saves a lot of searching later.

1. Click **New folder** to create a folder.
2. Give it a clear name (think "Projects," "Photos," or "Invoices").
3. Drag files and other folders inside to nest them.

To avoid sync issues, steer clear of illegal characters in filenames: `< > : " / \ | ? *`. On Windows, keep full file paths under **260 characters**.

If you ever delete something by accident, you can recover it from **Trash** within your retention window — **30 days** on Free, Plus, and Family, and **365 days** on Business.

## Share something

Sharing is where collaboration begins. Nimbus offers two main ways to share.

**Shared folders** let you invite people with a specific role:

- **Viewer** — can see files only
- **Commenter** — can view and comment
- **Editor** — can view, comment, and make changes
- **Manager** — full control, including managing access

**Shared links** let anyone with the URL view or edit, no invitation needed. On paid plans, you can add a **password** and an **expiry date** to a link for extra control.

To share a folder:

1. Select the folder and choose **Share**.
2. Enter email addresses and pick a role, or create a shareable link.
3. Send the invite or copy the link.

Need files from someone who doesn't have an account? Use a **file request** to let them upload directly to a folder you choose. For deeper collaboration, try **Nimbus Paper** for collaborative docs with comments and **@mentions**, and use comments and version history on your files to track changes.

## Install the mobile app

Take Nimbus with you by installing the app on **iOS or Android**.

1. Download the Nimbus app from the App Store or Google Play.
2. Sign in with your account.
3. Enable the features that matter most to you.

On mobile, you can:

- Turn on **auto camera upload** to back up photos and videos automatically — with an optional **Wi-Fi-only** setting to save mobile data.
- Mark files as **"Available offline"** so you can open them without a connection.
- Use the **document scanner** (Plus and above) to turn photos into multi-page PDFs.
- Add an **app passcode or biometric lock** for an extra layer of privacy.

Note that mobile uploads count toward your storage.

## Get help

You're all set! If you ever run into trouble, **Free, Plus, and Family** users can reach support by **email and chat** (typically 24–48 hours), and **Business** plans include priority support. You can also check live service updates at **status.nimbus.example**.
