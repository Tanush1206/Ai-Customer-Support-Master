# Using the Nimbus Mobile App

The Nimbus mobile app for iOS and Android lets you access your files on the go, back up your photos automatically, keep important documents available without a connection, scan paper into PDFs, and lock the app behind a passcode. This guide walks through the main mobile features and how to set them up.

## Camera Upload

Camera upload automatically backs up the photos and videos on your device to Nimbus, so your memories are saved even if you lose your phone. Uploaded media is stored in your Nimbus account alongside your other files.

To turn on camera upload:

1. Open the Nimbus app and go to **Settings**.
2. Find the **Camera upload** option and toggle it on.
3. Grant the app permission to access your photos and videos if prompted.

Once enabled, new photos and videos are uploaded in the background as you take them.

### Wi-Fi-only uploads

If you want to avoid using your cellular data plan, enable the **Wi-Fi-only** option in the camera upload settings. With this turned on, Nimbus pauses uploads when you are on a mobile connection and resumes them automatically once you reconnect to Wi-Fi.

> Note: Photos and videos uploaded from your phone count toward your account storage, just like any other file. Keep an eye on your usage if you are on the Free plan (5 GB).

## Offline Files

By default, the mobile app streams files on demand, which keeps your device storage free. When you know you will be somewhere without a connection — a flight, the subway, or a remote site — you can mark specific files to keep on your device.

To make a file available offline:

1. Browse to the file you want.
2. Tap the **More** (•••) menu next to it.
3. Select **Available offline**.

The file downloads to your device and stays accessible even with no internet connection. Any edits you make while offline sync back to Nimbus the next time you are online.

To free up space later, open the same menu and turn **Available offline** back off. This removes the local copy while leaving the file safely stored in Nimbus.

## Document Scanner

The document scanner turns photos of paper documents into clean, multi-page PDFs that are saved straight to Nimbus. It is great for receipts, contracts, whiteboards, and handwritten notes.

The document scanner is available on the **Plus** plan and above.

To scan a document:

1. Tap the **+** (create/upload) button in the app.
2. Choose **Scan document**.
3. Position your device over the page and capture it. The app detects the edges of the document automatically.
4. To build a multi-page PDF, tap **Add page** and capture each additional page.
5. When you are finished, save the scan to a folder in Nimbus.

Your scanned PDF is then available across all your devices and can be shared like any other file.

## Passcode and Biometric Lock

To keep your files private if your phone is lost, stolen, or borrowed, you can require a passcode or biometric check every time the Nimbus app opens.

To enable app lock:

1. Open **Settings** in the app.
2. Select the **Passcode** or app lock option.
3. Set a numeric passcode.
4. If your device supports it, enable **biometric unlock** (such as fingerprint or face recognition) for faster access.

After this is set up, the Nimbus app stays locked behind your passcode or biometrics, adding a layer of protection on top of your account password and two-factor authentication.

## Mobile Storage Usage

It helps to remember that everything you upload from your phone — camera roll backups, scanned documents, and files you create or save — counts toward your overall account storage. Storage is shared across all of your files, backups, and Nimbus Paper docs.

Here is how much storage each plan includes:

- **Free:** 5 GB
- **Plus and Family:** 2 TB
- **Business:** 5 TB pooled per user

A few tips for managing space from your phone:

- Turn off **Available offline** for files you no longer need on the device. This reclaims local device space without affecting your cloud storage.
- If you are filling up your account, deleted files can be recovered from **Trash** within your retention window — 30 days on Free, Plus, and Family, and 365 days on Business.
- On the Free plan, consider enabling **Wi-Fi-only** camera upload and reviewing large videos, since media files add up quickly against the 5 GB limit.

## Getting Help

If something is not working as expected, check the Nimbus status page at **status.nimbus.example**. Free, Plus, and Family customers can reach support by email and chat, with a typical response time of 24 to 48 hours. Business plans include priority support, and Enterprise customers have phone support and a dedicated Customer Success Manager.
