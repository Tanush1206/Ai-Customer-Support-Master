# Resolved Support Tickets — Billing

This page collects real, resolved billing tickets from the Nimbus support queue. Each example shows the customer's issue and how our team resolved it, so you can find a fix for a similar situation on your own account.

Most billing questions can be handled from **Settings → Billing**, where you can update your card, view invoices, change plans, and cancel.

## How to read these tickets

Each entry includes:

- **Ticket #** — the reference number
- **Customer issue** — the problem as the customer described it
- **Resolution** — the steps our team took, and what you can do too

## Ticket #4821 — Charged twice in the same month

### Customer issue

"I see two charges from Nimbus on my card for my Plus plan this month. I only have one account and I didn't sign up twice."

### Resolution

We reviewed the account's invoices under **Settings → Billing → Invoices**. A duplicate charge most often comes from one of two things: an upgrade that was prorated immediately, or a second account billed to the same card.

1. Open **Settings → Billing** and select **Invoices**.
2. Compare the line items on each invoice. An **upgrade** (for example, Free or Plus to a higher plan) is prorated and billed right away, which can look like a second charge in the same period.
3. If both invoices are genuine duplicates for the same plan and period, reply to the ticket with both invoice numbers.

In this case, one charge was the regular Plus renewal and the other was a prorated upgrade. We confirmed the proration was correct and explained the math to the customer. Where a true duplicate occurs, we reverse the extra charge.

## Ticket #4907 — Refund request after recent upgrade

### Customer issue

"I just upgraded to Plus a few days ago and realized I don't need it. Can I get my money back?"

### Resolution

Nimbus offers a **14-day money-back refund on the first purchase of a paid plan**. Because this was the customer's first paid purchase and the request came within 14 days, the refund was approved.

1. Reply to the ticket (or open a new one from chat/email) confirming you want the refund.
2. We verify the purchase date and that it is your first paid plan.
3. Once approved, the charge is reversed and the account returns to **Free** (5 GB storage, 1 user, 30-day version history and trash, basic sharing links).

A reminder for anyone in this situation: the 14-day money-back guarantee applies to the **first** purchase of a paid plan. After that window, you can still cancel anytime and keep access until the end of your billing period.

## Ticket #5034 — Confused about a prorated charge

### Customer issue

"I changed plans mid-cycle and the amount I was charged doesn't match the sticker price. Why is it a strange number?"

### Resolution

This is expected behavior. **Upgrades are prorated immediately** — you pay only for the remaining days in your current billing period at the new plan's rate, which is why the charge is rarely a round number.

To verify a prorated charge:

1. Go to **Settings → Billing → Invoices**.
2. Open the most recent invoice and read the line items, which show the prorated adjustment for the days remaining in the cycle.

It also helps to know the difference in direction:

- **Upgrades** apply immediately and are prorated.
- **Downgrades** apply at your **next renewal**, not right away — so you keep your current plan's features until the period ends.

We walked the customer through their invoice line items and confirmed the prorated amount was correct.

## Ticket #5188 — Switching from monthly to annual billing

### Customer issue

"I'm on a monthly plan and want to switch to annual to save money. How do I do that and how much do I save?"

### Resolution

Annual billing saves roughly **17% — about two months free** compared to paying monthly. For reference:

- **Plus:** $11.99/mo, or $119.88/yr ($9.99/mo billed annually)
- **Family:** $19.99/mo, or $199.88/yr
- **Business:** $20/user/mo monthly, or $18/user/mo billed annually (minimum 3 users)

To switch:

1. Open **Settings → Billing**.
2. Choose the annual option for your plan.
3. Confirm the change. Because moving to annual is treated as an upgrade to a longer term, it applies immediately and is prorated against what you've already paid for the current period.

We confirmed the customer's new annual rate and pointed out that the per-month effective price drops (for example, Plus from $11.99/mo to $9.99/mo billed annually).

## Ticket #5252 — Want to cancel but keep access until the period ends

### Customer issue

"I'd like to cancel my subscription, but I've already paid for this month. Do I lose access immediately, or can I keep using my plan until it runs out?"

### Resolution

You keep your paid features until the end of the period you've already paid for. **When you cancel, access continues until the period end** — it does not stop the moment you cancel.

1. Go to **Settings → Billing**.
2. Select **Cancel**.
3. Your plan stays active with all its features until your current billing period ends, after which the account moves to **Free**.

Before the downgrade takes effect, it's worth checking your storage. Free includes **5 GB**, and storage is shared across all your files, backups, and Nimbus Paper docs. If you're above that limit when the plan ends, plan ahead by removing or moving files.

We confirmed the cancellation, noted the date access would continue through, and reminded the customer they can resubscribe anytime.

## A note on failed payments

Separate from cancellations: if a payment fails, Nimbus **retries for 7 days** before the account is downgraded to Free. If you hit this, update your card under **Settings → Billing** to avoid losing access to your plan's features.
