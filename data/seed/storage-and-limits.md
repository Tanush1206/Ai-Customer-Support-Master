# Storage, Limits & Trash

Nimbus gives every account a storage allowance plus generous limits for file sizes, version history, and shared-link traffic. This guide explains how much space you get on each plan, what counts toward it, how long deleted files and old versions are kept, and how to recover something you've removed.

## Storage quotas by plan

Your storage is the total space available for everything you keep in Nimbus across Web, the Desktop app, and Mobile.

- **Free** — 5 GB
- **Plus** — 2 TB
- **Family** — 2 TB per member (up to 6 members)
- **Business** — 5 TB pooled per user
- **Enterprise** — custom, with optional unlimited storage

On Business plans, storage is pooled across the team, so members who use less leave more room for those who need it. On Family, each of the up to six members has their own 2 TB.

To check your current usage, open **Settings → Billing**, where you can also upgrade if you're running low. Upgrades are prorated and take effect immediately.

## What counts toward your storage

Storage is shared across all of your content. The following all draw from the same allowance:

- Files and folders you upload or sync
- Backups
- Nimbus Paper docs
- Mobile uploads, including auto camera uploads of photos and videos

If you're approaching your limit, removing large files from Trash (after the retention window, or by emptying it) and clearing out old content will free space.

## Maximum file size

There's a cap on how large any single file can be. The limit depends on your plan:

- **Free** — 2 GB per file
- **Plus** and **Family** — 50 GB per file
- **Business** — 100 GB per file

A file that exceeds your plan's maximum can't be uploaded or synced, even if you have plenty of total storage remaining. If you need to move very large files to someone else, Plus and Family include **Nimbus Transfer** for sending up to 100 GB.

## Version history & trash retention

Nimbus automatically keeps previous versions of your files and holds onto deleted items for a set period so you can roll back changes or recover something you removed by mistake. Version history and Trash use the same retention window:

- **Free** — 30 days
- **Plus** — 30 days for Trash; 180-day version history
- **Family** — 30 days for Trash; 180-day version history
- **Business** — 365 days

After the retention window passes, deleted files and old versions are permanently removed and can no longer be recovered.

## Recovering deleted files

As long as you're still within your plan's retention window, you can restore deleted files from Trash:

1. Sign in to Nimbus on the Web.
2. Open **Trash** from the sidebar.
3. Locate the file or folder you want to bring back.
4. Select it and choose **Restore**.
5. The item returns to its original location.

If you deleted a file but only need an earlier version of a file that still exists, use **version history** on that file instead and restore the version you want.

Files deleted beyond the retention window are gone permanently, so restore anything important before its window closes.

## Shared-link daily traffic limits

When you share files using shared links, there's a limit on how much data those links can serve in a single day. This protects everyone's account from runaway traffic on popular links. The daily allowance depends on your plan:

- **Free** — 20 GB per day
- **Plus** and **Family** — 1 TB per day
- **Business** — 4 TB per day

This limit applies to traffic generated by people downloading or viewing content through your shared links. If a link is heavily accessed and reaches the daily cap, traffic may be limited until the next day. Paid plans also let you add **passwords and expiry dates** to links for tighter control over who can access them and for how long.

## Frequently asked questions

### Does emptying Trash free up storage right away?

Items in Trash still count while they're within the retention window. Permanently removing them—either by emptying Trash or letting the window lapse—frees the space they used.

### Why can't I upload a large file even though I have space left?

Each plan has a per-file maximum (2 GB on Free, 50 GB on Plus and Family, 100 GB on Business). A file above that cap can't be uploaded regardless of your remaining total storage.

### How do I get more storage?

Upgrade to a higher plan in **Settings → Billing**. Upgrades are prorated and apply immediately, so your new quota is available right away.

### Can I recover a file after the retention window ends?

No. Once the retention window passes, deleted files and previous versions are permanently erased. Restore important items from Trash, or pull the version you need from version history, before the window closes.
