# Nimbus Integrations

Nimbus connects with the tools your team already uses, so you can share files, get notifications, and automate work without leaving your other apps. This guide explains the available integrations, how to connect each one, where to manage them, and the admin controls available on Business plans.

## Available integrations

Nimbus currently supports the following integrations:

- **Slack** — Share Nimbus links and receive notifications directly in your Slack channels and direct messages.
- **Google Workspace** — Open and edit Google Docs stored in Nimbus.
- **Microsoft 365** — Co-author Office files (such as Word, Excel, and PowerPoint) without leaving Nimbus.
- **Zoom** — Share your Zoom recordings to Nimbus.
- **Zapier** — Build automated workflows that connect Nimbus to thousands of other apps.

## Where to manage integrations

You connect, review, and disconnect integrations from one place:

1. Sign in to Nimbus on the web.
2. Open **Settings → Integrations**.
3. Browse the list of available integrations and connect or disconnect them as needed.

This page is the central hub for all of your connected services, so it's the best starting point whenever you want to add a new integration or remove one you no longer use.

## Connect Slack

The Slack integration lets you share Nimbus links and receive notifications in Slack.

1. Go to **Settings → Integrations**.
2. Find **Slack** in the list and select it.
3. Follow the prompts to authorize Nimbus to connect to your Slack workspace.

Once connected, you can share Nimbus links into Slack and get notifications about your files where your team is already working.

## Connect Google Workspace

Connecting Google Workspace lets you open and edit Google Docs that live in Nimbus.

1. Go to **Settings → Integrations**.
2. Select **Google Workspace**.
3. Authorize the connection when prompted.

After connecting, Google Docs stored in Nimbus open directly in Google's editor, so you can keep your documents in Nimbus while editing them in the familiar Google interface.

## Connect Microsoft 365

The Microsoft 365 integration enables real-time co-authoring of Office files stored in Nimbus.

1. Go to **Settings → Integrations**.
2. Select **Microsoft 365**.
3. Complete the authorization steps when prompted.

With Microsoft 365 connected, you and your collaborators can co-author Office files together, with changes reflected as you work.

## Connect Zoom

Use the Zoom integration to bring your meeting recordings into Nimbus.

1. Go to **Settings → Integrations**.
2. Select **Zoom**.
3. Authorize Nimbus to connect to your Zoom account.

Once connected, you can share your Zoom recordings to Nimbus, keeping your recordings alongside your other files for easy storage and sharing.

## Connect Zapier

Zapier lets you automate tasks between Nimbus and other apps without writing code.

1. Go to **Settings → Integrations**.
2. Select **Zapier**.
3. Follow the steps to authorize the connection.

With Zapier connected, you can build automated workflows that link Nimbus to the other services in your stack.

## Admin restrictions on Business

On the **Business** plan, administrators have additional control over which integrations team members can use. Business admins can restrict which integrations are allowed across the organization.

This means that even though the full list of integrations appears under **Settings → Integrations**, the options available to an individual team member depend on what the admin has permitted. If you're on a Business plan and don't see an integration you expect, or you're unable to connect one, your administrator may have restricted it.

Business admins manage these controls as part of the admin console, which is included with the Business plan along with other team management tools.

### For team members

- The integrations you can connect reflect your organization's policy.
- If you need an integration that isn't available, contact your Nimbus administrator to request access.

### For Business admins

- Use the admin console to set which integrations are allowed for your team.
- Restricting integrations applies organization-wide, helping you keep your team's connected services aligned with your security and compliance needs.

## Managing and removing integrations

You can review your connected integrations at any time and disconnect any you no longer need:

1. Open **Settings → Integrations**.
2. Locate the integration you want to manage.
3. Disconnect it to revoke Nimbus's access to that service.

It's good practice to periodically review your connected integrations and remove any you're no longer using.

## Need help?

If you run into trouble connecting an integration:

- Confirm you're signed in to Nimbus and have access to **Settings → Integrations**.
- On Business plans, check with your administrator in case the integration has been restricted.
- Check the Nimbus status page at **status.nimbus.example** to confirm there are no ongoing service issues.

For further assistance, reach out to Nimbus support. Support response times and channels vary by plan: Free, Plus, and Family plans include email and chat support, Business includes priority support, and Enterprise adds phone support with a dedicated Customer Success Manager.
